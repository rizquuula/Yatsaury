The Life of the Prophet Muhammad (Peace Be Upon Him)

Muhammad ibn Abdullah was born in Makkah, in the Arabian Peninsula, around 570 CE,
in the Year of the Elephant — so named because Abraha's army, which included war elephants,
marched on Makkah that year but was miraculously repelled.

His father, Abdullah ibn Abd al-Muttalib, died before his birth, and his mother,
Aminah bint Wahb, passed away when he was just six years old. He was then raised
by his grandfather, Abd al-Muttalib, and later by his uncle, Abu Talib.

From an early age, Muhammad was known for his honesty and trustworthiness.
The people of Makkah called him Al-Amin, meaning "the trustworthy one."
He worked as a merchant and was hired by Khadijah bint Khuwaylid to manage her trade caravans.
Impressed by his character and integrity, Khadijah proposed marriage, and they wed when
Muhammad was twenty-five years old and she was forty.

At the age of forty, in 610 CE, Muhammad received the first revelation from Allah
through the Angel Jibreel (Gabriel) in the Cave of Hira on Mount Nur, near Makkah.
The first words revealed were: "Read in the name of your Lord who created."

This marked the beginning of his prophethood and the revelation of the Quran,
the holy scripture of Islam, which was completed over the next twenty-three years.
